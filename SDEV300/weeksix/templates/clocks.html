Where did time come from?

Cavemen

Human beings first noticed the passage of time by the passage of the sun, moon and stars in the prehistoric era some 30,000 years ago.

Babylonians

The ancient Babylonians first developed a mathematical system based on the number 60 and a year of 360 days, by around 1600 B.C.E.

Egyptians

Ancient Egyptians were known to have built sundials as early as 1500 B.C.E. In ancient times, basing the measurement of time on celestial bodies was seasonal.

Galileo

With the invention of the pendulum by Galileo Galilei, in the 17th century, the concept of time began to change and become more precise and consistent.

Time.gov

The US Government hosts a website which has the official time for the entire country. Click the map to visit the site now!

Network Time

Network Time Protocol or NTP helps computers stay on the same time so that they can connect correctly. Click the picture to find an NTP server!
